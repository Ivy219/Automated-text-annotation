However, current national pledges to reduce emissions are insufficient to meet this target, and many experts warn that the world is on track to exceed the 1.5-degree threshold by the mid-2030s.

Guterres emphasized that the solution to the climate crisis lies in a rapid transition to renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power, and the electrification of transportation and industry. He also highlighted the need for increased investment in sustainable infrastructure, energy efficiency, and climate-resilient agriculture.

The UN chief also emphasized the need for a just transition, which prioritizes the needs of vulnerable communities and workers who may be disproportionately affected by the shift to a low-carbon economy. He urged governments to provide support for workers in industries that are likely to be impacted by the transition, such as coal mining and fossil fuel extraction.

The summit also saw the launch of several new initiatives aimed at accelerating the transition to a low-carbon economy. These included a global alliance of cities committed to achieving net-zero emissions, a new climate-focused investment fund, and a global initiative to promote sustainable agriculture practices.

Despite the sense of urgency and frustration that pervaded the summit, there were also signs of hope and optimism. Many attendees highlighted the progress that has been made in recent years, including the rapid decline in the cost of renewable energy and the growth of the global clean energy sector.
